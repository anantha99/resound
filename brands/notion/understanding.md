# Notion Understanding

## What We Sell

Notion is an all-in-one workspace for docs, wikis, projects, databases, and AI
workflows. Users range from individual productivity fans to enterprise teams.

## Voice And Positioning

Signals often contrast product love with reliability expectations. Enterprise
buyers treat uptime, security, pricing, and migration risk as high-severity.

## Functional Area Taxonomy

- product: databases, AI, templates, collaboration, mobile/editor UX.
- engineering: outages, sync issues, latency, data loss, status page accuracy.
- billing: pricing, renewal increases, plan limits, seat management.
- cs: support responsiveness, migration help, enterprise onboarding.
- marketing: positioning, competitive comparisons, launch reception.
- ops: internal/process issues surfaced by customers.
- other: anything that does not fit above.

## Glossary

- Notion AI: AI features inside the workspace; positive signals can feed content.
- Confluence, Coda, Obsidian, Linear: competitor or replacement mentions.
- Status page lag: reliability trust issue; route to incident/comms lane.

## Examples

- "Three outages in two weeks; IT wants Confluence" → area=engineering,
  subarea=reliability, severity=critical, action_class=immediate.
- "Renewal jumped 40% with no new features" → area=billing, severity=high,
  action_class=sprint.
- "Notion AI Q&A over my docs is incredible" → area=product,
  sentiment=positive, action_class=fyi.
- "I use a notion of fairness..." without product context → is_about_brand=false,
  action_class=ignore.

## Severity Guidance

- critical: outage, data loss, security claim, viral enterprise churn risk.
- high: repeated reliability issue, pricing shock, migration threat.
- medium: recurring UX/product complaint with limited reach.
- low: praise, minor issue, or informational mention.

## Quick notes on why this is structured this way

- **Leads with the role, not with you.** Recruiters get hundreds of "I love your company" messages. Naming the specific opening signals you've done your homework and aren't spraying applications.
- **Resound earns its place.** You mentioned you wanted to talk about what you're building — but only as evidence that you do the *kind of work* the role asks for. A product the recruiter has never heard of isn't a credential; what you did with it is.
- **"I like solving problems" is shown, not stated.** The line you wanted — that you like solving problems and building solutions — is implied throughout (the user-loop description, the "rather ship rough than perfect" line). Stating it directly makes you sound like every other applicant; demonstrating it makes you sound credible.
- **Acknowledges the PM-vs-Consultant gap honestly.** Pretending you only want Product Consultant when you actually want PM reads as fake; pretending you'll skip Product Consultant straight to PM reads as arrogant. Naming it out loud — "Consultant is the right starting point because it forces you to earn the instincts" — disarms the issue.
- **No fluff closer.** "Looking forward to hearing from you" is invisible. A specific ask (20 min, here's what I'll share) gets a higher response rate.
